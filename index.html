I. Strategic Framework & Project Charter

This section outlines the project's core mission, the principles that will guide our work, and the defined scope, ensuring every task aligns with our strategic objectives and contractual commitments.

1.1. Mission Statement

To develop and deliver a single, comprehensive, evidence-informed TK-12 digital citizenship and literacy curriculum that is uniquely tailored to the needs of California's diverse student population, empowering them to navigate the digital world safely, ethically, and responsibly.

1.3. Scope of Work at a Glance

  • Core Curriculum: Develop 3 to 5 core modules, differentiated for three grade bands (TK-5, 6-8, 9-12).
  • Training Materials: Create 9 to 15 sample lesson plans (one per module, per grade band).
  • Foundational Research: Produce a 10-20 page Landscape Analysis & Literature Review.
  • Dissemination Support: Develop co-branded communications materials to support project rollout.

1.2. Guiding Principles: Our Value Proposition

Equity-Centered Lens

Explicitly address the digital experiences and technology access disparities of California's diverse student populations.

Restorative Practices

Promote wellness and strengthen school climates as alternatives to exclusionary discipline for tech-related behaviors.

Mental Health Integration

Promote healthy media habits and support student well-being in digital spaces, in service of CDE initiatives.

Rigorous, Research-Based

Built on a foundation of scientific evidence from developmental psychology and neuroscience.

4.3. Risk Management & Mitigation

Risk 1: Political Sensitivities

The educational landscape is highly politicized. Terms like "equity" and "SEL" can be contentious.

Mitigation: Use CDPH Kickoff to gain explicit guidance on sensitive topics. Iish Ryaru will provide ongoing review to ensure framing is politically viable.

Risk 2: Compressed Timeline

The final feedback and revision window (June 1-30, 2026) is extremely narrow.

Mitigation: Utilize parallel workstreams in Phase 3. Phase 4 is reserved exclusively for CDPH feedback integration. Strict adherence to the May 31 draft submission deadline is critical.
